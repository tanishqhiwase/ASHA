Indian Government Health Schemes

Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PMJAY)

Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PMJAY), also known as Ayushman Bharat Yojana, is a flagship scheme launched by the Government of India to provide health insurance coverage to economically vulnerable families.

The scheme aims to provide coverage of up to INR 5 lakh per family per year for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization across public and empanelled private hospitals in India.

PMJAY covers over 10 crore families identified as deprived rural families and occupational category of urban workers' families, as per the Socio-Economic Caste Census (SECC) data.

Beneficiaries under PMJAY can avail cashless and paperless treatment at any of the empanelled hospitals, and the scheme covers a wide range of medical treatments and procedures.

PMJAY aims to reduce out-of-pocket expenditure on healthcare and ensure access to quality healthcare services for the economically disadvantaged sections of society.

Mukhyamantri Amrutam Yojana

Launched by the government of Gujarat, the Mukhyamantri Amrutam Yojana initially catered to families and individuals living under the poverty line. However, after many considerations and amendments, the benefits of the policy were also extended to people belonging to the lower income group.

The major benefit of this scheme is that it covers the complete medical expenditure from diagnostic examinations to post-hospitalization care. It also covers a wide range of medical ailments whose treatments will be fully funded by the government.

A sum insured of INR 3 lakh is provided to all policyholders, however in cases of special treatments such as kidney, liver or pancreas transplant, the sum insured increases to Rs. 5 lakhs. An important thing to note is that a maximum of five people in every family can enroll for this scheme.

Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jan Arogya Yojana

The Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jan Arogya Yojana was curated by the government of Maharashtra for the domiciles of the state. The primary goal is to provide cashless medical facilities and quality healthcare to people of lower income groups.

The policy covers an extensive list of 971 medical procedures with additional 120 post therapy packages. These also include orthopedic surgeries, gynecologic and obstetric treatments, cardiac surgeries, radiation surgeries, as well as plastic surgeries among many others.

While the sum insured amount per family is just Rs. 1.50 lakhs for every year which is low, the benefits of the scheme are diverse. The insurance premium is borne by the state. Medical benefits also cover consultation charges along with diagnostic care expenses. Apart from government hospitals, there are several private hospitals that consider the policy and offer treatments to the beneficiaries.

Central Government Health Scheme (CGHS)

The Central Government Health Scheme was launched in 1954 and primarily caters to officials working with the central government such as supreme court judges and central railway officials. The policy also includes pensioners.

The policy aims to provide hospitalization and domiciliary care including medication from allopathy as well as homeopathy. This scheme also provides free X-ray and laboratory examination facilities to central government officials.

West Bengal Health Scheme

West Bengal has been commended many times for its prioritization of bettering public health infrastructure. In this regard, taking the West Bengal health scheme could be a life-saver. However, this can only be availed by people in All India Services or pensioners.

This scheme provides treatment costs for at least 1014 medical ailments including OPD treatments. The key features of the policy include cashless claims, reimbursements for treatments in non-empanelled hospitals, OPD treatment coverages, and out of hospitalization charges coverage.

Yeshasvini Health Insurance Scheme

Considering the plight of farmers, lower income and middle income groups in the state of Karnataka, the state government along with development co-operatives sponsor the Yeshasvini Health Insurance Scheme. This policy is meant to provide high quality yet affordable care in Karnataka.

The sum insured for policyholders is INR 5 lakh along with an added advantage of cashless claims at network hospitals. The scheme also includes claim benefits for 1,650 diseases and one can also get discounts on certain diagnostic or treatment costs.

Karunya Health Scheme

The Karunya Health Scheme focuses on providing healthcare access for people of Kerala facing critical illness. People of the lower income groups can avail this policy to get medical facilities for chronic ailments including cancer, cardiovascular issues and palliative care amongst many.

The sum insured for the Karunya Health Scheme is INR 5 lakh per family. The coverage includes expenditures for diagnostic care, consultations, pre and post hospitalization charges, and intensive care treatments.

Telangana State Government Employees and Journalists Health Scheme

A unique insurance policy curated by the Telangana state government is the EJHS or Employees and Journalists Health Scheme. The policy, as the name suggests, promotes healthcare and welfare for its professionals including journalists and their families. The policy entitles them to avail cashless hospitalization coverage across network hospitals in the state.

The benefits include in-patient and out-patient treatment, hospital stay, as well as follow up services. The coverage in the policy does not have any upper limit.

Employment State Insurance Scheme

One of the oldest health insurance policies formulated by the government of India, the Employment State Insurance Scheme provides coverage for working professionals. Usually, a company with more than 10 employees should register itself for the ESI scheme.

This scheme includes a wide range of health-related contingencies and also offers pension options to the family in cases of death or disablement due to occupational hazards. The policy includes coverage for sickness, maternity, disability, unemployment, funeral costs, and old age medical care amongst others.

Dr YSR Aarogyasri Health Care Trust

An initiative by the government of Andhra Pradesh, the Dr YSR Aarogyasri Health Care Trust insures the people of the state who fall in lower economic strata. This health care insurance includes a variety of benefits including maternity coverage, rehabilitation services, cashless claims, as well as continuity of care from diagnosis to post treatment care.

The policy has a strong focus on preventive measures of healthcare which includes free health check-ups and screenings for certain ailments. The policy also covers treatments for numerous chronic diseases including chemotherapy, radiation therapy and dialysis.
